QUESTIONS AND ANSWERS

General FAQs

  • Does Academic Center provide services in person or virtually?

    Both! The Academic Center provides students with the option to choose the format in which they would like to engage with the Center. Students can meet with an academic coach or a peer tutor in person or virtually via Zoom, and they can choose to attend in-person workshops or virtual workshops. College students can attend accountability hours in person or virtually via Zoom.

  • Where is the Academic Center located?

    Please visit us in our new location inside Roy O. West Library! We are in room 102 inside The Learning Commons, just to the right as you enter the East entrance doors.

  • When is the Academic Center open?

    The Academic Center is open from 9:00 a.m. to 5:00 p.m. Monday through Friday. Students can view the available times for Academic Center services (academic coaching, accountability groups, peer tutoring, and workshops) on the Academic Scheduler.

  • Who should go to the ARC?

    All DePauw students are welcome at Academic Center. Whether you are a first-year student or about to graduate, the ARC has something for you: we believe in continual learning and improvement, and we believe that everyone can benefit from academic support.

  • Why should I come to the Academic Center?

    Would you like to get more out of your classes? Read more effectively, finish your assignments with time to spare? Would you like to find that “aha!” feeling more often? Would you like to feel in charge of your schedule (and not the other way around)? The ARC is here to help with this and more – come see us to learn more!

  • What services does Academic Center offer?

    We offer Speaking tutor, Q tutor for STEM courses, and Writing tutor for writing assigment.

W Tutors can help you get started on a writing assignment, work with you on revisions.

Writing Center tutors are well-prepared to give you feedback and suggestions for any writing assignment. Visit the W Center to talk over essays that you're working on, no matter what the discipline. Students enrolled in writing-intensive classes, such as First-Year Seminars, W Courses, and Senior Seminars, may find it especially helpful to discuss assignments and essay drafts with a W Tutor.

Q Tutors are ready to help you solve quantitative reasoning problems.

Q Tutors are ready to help you solve quantitative reasoning problems in Mathematics, Logic, Psychology, Chemistry & Biochemistry, Biology, Geology, Physics & Astronomy, Economics, Statistics, and Computer Science.

S Center consultants can help you practice your oral presentations, interviews, and class discussion.

S Center consultants are highly trained individuals who specialize in helping individuals improve their oral communication skills. Whether you are preparing for a formal speech, job interview, class discussion, or any type of performance that involves speaking, these consultants can provide valuable guidance and support to enhance your presentation abilities.
